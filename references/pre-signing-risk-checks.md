# Pre-Signing Risk Checks

Use this file when the user is about to sign a lease, pay a deposit, pay a transfer fee, take over a shop, share a shop, borrow another license/account, or start with partners/family.

## Required User Facts

Ask for:

- Stage: only looking, deposit paid, lease signed, transfer agreement signed, renovation started, or already open.
- Landlord chain: first landlord, second landlord, mall/property company, previous tenant, broker, and whether written consent exists.
- Lease terms: monthly rent, property fee, payment cycle, deposit, rent-free period, lease length, rent increase, allowed business scope, sublease/transfer/early-exit clause.
- Transfer package: transfer fee, remaining rent, deposit, movable equipment, inventory/materials, decoration, platform accounts, member balances, licenses, staff, recipes, supplier contacts.
- Compliance: business license, food operation permit/filing, fire safety, oil fume/exhaust, drainage, gas/electric capacity, outdoor seating, signboard, noise/night operation, alcohol or special category requirements.
- Partnership facts if any: each person's cash contribution, debt, labor, salary, decision power, equity, profit distribution, loss cap, exit rule, and who controls payment accounts.

## Lease And License Checks

Before signing or renovating, verify:

1. Whether this address can legally do the intended food category, especially heavy cooking, frying, barbecue, hot pot, alcohol, late-night, or outdoor seating.
2. Whether the landlord or mall/property company permits the category in writing.
3. Whether oil fume, drainage, grease trap, gas/electric load, fire passage, and exhaust route can pass local requirements.
4. Whether the business license and food permit/filing must be newly applied instead of "transferred" from the previous tenant.
5. Whether the lease allows assignment, sublease, early termination, decoration removal, deposit return, and signboard installation.
6. Whether a second landlord has written authority to lease and whether the first landlord/property company recognizes the contract.

If any of these are unclear, the default advice is pause signing and do not renovate.

## Transfer Fee Logic

Break the transfer price into five buckets:

1. Remaining rent and refundable deposit.
2. Movable equipment with resale value.
3. Inventory/materials that can actually be used.
4. Decoration residual value, usually much lower than the seller's cost.
5. Real goodwill: verified repeat customers, verified revenue, platform account value, and site scarcity.

Do not accept an "all-in package" number. Ask the user to price each bucket separately.

Minimum evidence before taking over:

- POS/cashier backend by day for 6-12 months, not screenshots for a few good days.
- WeChat/Alipay/bank/payment-platform records matching the cashier data.
- Delivery-platform backend, group-buying verification, refunds, coupons, and commission.
- Purchase invoices, utility bills, wage records, rent contract, and tax/fee clues.
- Observation at lunch/dinner/weekend peaks and competitor counts.
- Reason for transfer, previous owner working hours, staff stability, and whether key people leave after transfer.

Treat unexplained revenue spikes, bundled transfer price, seller refusing full backend data, high transfer fee in a weak site, or "near school/mall/station" without path evidence as pause signals.

## Partnership And Family Money

Treat partnership as a business design problem, not a relationship promise.

Before starting, require written answers to:

- Who is the real operator every day?
- Who owns the payment account, delivery account, supplier account, and license subject?
- Is owner labor paid as salary before profit distribution?
- What is the maximum loss each person can bear?
- Who can decide menu, hiring, price, discount, loans, and closure?
- How can a partner exit, and how are equipment, deposit, inventory, and debt valued on exit?

Red flags:

- Partners join because one person lacks money, not because capabilities complement each other.
- One partner provides "experience" but no verified profitable store data.
- Relatives or spouses mix personal spending, store cash, salary, and profit.
- A personal side project is embedded into another person's store, license, payment account, or delivery account.
- No one has final operating authority, but everyone can block decisions.

## If Already Signed Or Paid

Use stop-loss order:

1. Stop new payments, renovation, equipment orders, and irreversible demolition until the blocking issue is clear.
2. Preserve lease, transfer agreement, payment records, landlord authorization, chat logs, promised permits, photos, and storefront state.
3. Quantify the sunk loss of walking away now versus the larger loss after renovation/opening.
4. Negotiate withdrawal, rent-free extension, deposit return, category change, sublease, or transfer with written records.
5. If compliance, landlord authorization, or contract disputes remain unresolved, suggest local regulator/property/legal consultation.

Do not tell the user to "试一下" when the next step turns a small deposit into full rent, renovation, equipment, and inventory exposure.

## Output Pattern

```text
先说结论：现在不是能不能开的问题，是这几个签约前风险没核清。

我还缺：
1. 房东链条和合同主体
2. 租金/押金/付款周期/可否转租
3. 证照、油烟、消防、燃气/电量是否能办
4. 转让费拆分和真实流水证据
5. 合伙出资、工资、亏损和退出规则

勇哥式建议：没核清之前，不交大钱、不装修、不买设备。先把最坏损失锁在定金/押金层面。
```
