## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask category, city, current sales, gross margin.
2. Ask area, rent, labor, utilities and calculate daily/monthly loss.
3. Question headquarters' material subsidy: why ask a losing store to buy more materials?
4. Ask age, prior category experience, current occupation.
5. Ask how the store was acquired, who transferred it, how long the store had been open, and why headquarters transferred it.
6. Ask takeover price breakdown: deposit, rent, inventory, franchise fee, contract term.
7. Ask whether the user checked real data before taking over.
8. Calculate break-even: at 45% gross margin and fixed costs around 60,000/month, monthly sales need about 130,000; daily sales need about 4,400.
9. Inspect mall floor, traffic, neighboring stores, and best-performing categories.
10. Ask current thought and give transfer timing.

## Calculation Logic

- Daily gross profit at 3,000 sales and 45% margin is about 1,350.
- Daily fixed burden is rent 1,000 + labor 833 + utilities 166 = about 1,999.
- Daily loss is about 649; monthly loss is about 19,000-20,000.
- Break-even monthly sales are around 60,000 / 0.45 = 133,000, or about 4,400/day.
- Even the claimed first month of 110,000 did not reach break-even.

## Diagnosis

This is not a store to optimize slowly. It is a takeover trap:

- The previous owner/headquarters transferred risk to the user.
- The user treated embedded deposit/rent as value and underestimated the operating burden.
- The mall itself has weak traffic; 4th-floor corner position is not a strong fast-meal site.
- The category does not match a low-flow social餐饮 layer.
- Headquarters' "support" increases material purchase exposure instead of reducing fixed-cost pressure.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Transfer before the next high-traffic holiday window while the brand still has local awareness and the store still looks usable.
- Do not buy more headquarters materials just to receive a subsidy.
- Do not continue four more months hoping for optimization; the break-even gap and site limit are clear.
- In future takeover cases, require real sales records, platform backend, rent contract, deposit ownership, landlord consent, and reason for transfer before paying.

## Reusable Heuristics

- A cheap takeover often hides deposit, rent, and a dying store's problem.
- If the first month after takeover was already below break-even, later decay usually makes the case worse.
- A material recharge subsidy is not rescue; it can be a second sale to the victim.
- In a weak mall, the best category nearby matters more than headquarters' sales story.
