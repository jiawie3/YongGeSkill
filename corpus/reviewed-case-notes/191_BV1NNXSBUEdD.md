## User Facts Extracted

- Case type: planned opening, no payment yet, franchise/online-ad interception prevention.
- User profile: 37-year-old single mother with three children; eldest near high school, youngest in third grade.
- City: 云南曲靖会泽县.
- Prior work: long-term stationery/文具批发, with some old school/customer resources.
- Trigger: recently stopped/turned away from prior stationery shop and began watching short-video project ads.
- Planned category: milk tea, possibly with dessert/fried chicken/small snacks.
- Franchise source: saw brands on Douyin/short-video ads; had added several project-side contacts.
- Budget: initially thought 100,000 was enough, later realized likely 200,000+.
- Debt: opening would require borrowing about 100,000; 勇哥 warns it could become 200,000 debt if extra equipment/materials/decoration are added.
- Target site: 1st floor about 30 sqm plus forced 2nd floor about 300 sqm; rent quoted around 80,000-90,000/year; the real landlord/owner was not yet verified.
- Proposed labor: user plus two employees; local wages around 3,000/person, so labor about 9,000/month.
- Utilities assumption: about 3,000/month.
- Gross margin assumption: milk tea about 45%.
- Break-even estimate in dialogue: about 1,444/day.
- Competitive environment: nearby known milk tea/tea brands already exist, including 蜜雪/古茗/书亦-like competitors.

## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask city, age, family burden, number and age of children.
2. Ask why she wants to open milk tea and whether this comes from real demand or online ads.
3. Ask site area, rent, whether upstairs must be rented, and why the landlord/second landlord forces useless space.
4. Ask intended brand, where it was found, franchise fee, what equipment/materials are truly included.
5. Stress-test "5万全包": what if equipment is missing, materials are incomplete, or extra purchases are required?
6. Ask whether opening creates debt and how much.
7. Calculate staff, utilities, gross margin, and break-even.
8. Ask how many project contacts she has added and what categories: milk tea, coffee, bakery, snacks, health tea.
9. Interrupt the lead-funnel: block contacts, change phone/SIM if necessary, stop scrolling project ads.
10. Inspect competition and explain that business starts from demand and competition, not from ads.

## Calculation Logic

- Monthly fixed costs: labor about 9,000 + utilities about 3,000 + rent roughly 6,700-7,500/month = about 18,700-19,500/month.
- At 45% gross margin, break-even sales are about 41,500-43,300/month, around 1,400-1,450/day.
- This break-even ignores loan pressure, owner salary, platform fees, packaging, wastage, and extra headquarters purchases.
- A first-time debtor with three children cannot treat "maybe I can sell" as enough proof.

## Diagnosis

This is a pre-loss prevention case:

- The user is financially vulnerable and emotionally searching for a way out.
- Short-video ads created an illusion of opportunity.
- The planned category is one of the most competitive head-effect categories.
- The site has forced useless upstairs area and unverified landlord/second-landlord risk.
- The user's stationery network may be useful for a small product test, but not enough to justify a full shop.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not sign, do not pay, do not keep talking to the project sellers.
- Block the project contacts and stop scrolling franchise ads; if necessary, change phone/SIM to avoid further sales pressure.
- Do not open milk tea, coffee, hamburger, or bakery as a first debt-funded project. 勇哥 frames these as high-risk head-effect/loss categories for ordinary first-time owners.
- If testing the old stationery-channel idea, do it from home or a tiny batch: make/source a product, place it through old school/stationery contacts, and test real sell-through without opening a store.
- If considering any business near her own property later, evaluate the actual demand, foot traffic, competitors, and just-demand gaps first.

## Reusable Heuristics

- "我最近每天刷项目广告" is a high-risk intake signal; stop the lead funnel before discussing store plans.
- For vulnerable users with family/debt pressure, the first recommendation may be "disconnect from salespeople," not "do more research."
- Cheap franchise fees are often only the first hook; ask whether every piece of equipment, tool, material, decoration, and second purchase is truly included.
- Existing relationships can justify a no-store test, not a debt-funded shop.
- Business opportunity is demand plus unmet gap plus ability, not a video ad plus emotional urgency.
