## Calculation Logic

- Fixed cost: rent 23,000 + labor 22,000 + utilities 3,000-4,000 = about 48,000-49,000/month.
- At 70% gross margin, break-even sales are roughly 69,000-70,000/month, about 2,285/day.
- This break-even ignores many realities: wastage, delivery platform fees, discounts, packaging, owner time, and opening inefficiency.
- To earn the hoped 50,000-60,000/month, the store must do far more than break even, likely well above the user's already-optimistic 4,000-5,000/day.
- The transfer fee was not paid, so stopping now saves the largest avoidable loss.

## Diagnosis

The case is a classic expansion illusion:

- The user has one old store that benefited from timing, location, and accumulated old customers; that does not prove repeatable expansion ability.
- Prior failed branches already showed weak multi-store capability, but the user attributed failure mostly to疫情/外卖 rather than ability and site discipline.
- The new point has人流, but not proven客流. Commuters passing through a city-village entrance may be in a hurry and not in a 20+ yuan fresh-juice mindset.
- A 20-30 yuan fresh juice is directly fighting cheaper, more familiar tea-drink options nearby.
- "I can make the product" is not the same as "I can make this site buy it".
- Partners may be investing in the owner's old-store reputation, not in a verified new-store model.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not sign/pay transfer fee. Stop this site.
- Keep and improve the existing profitable old store; adding 10,000/month profit there is better than risking 300,000-400,000 new investment.
- If friends want to invest/open, teach them the product and let them take their own risk; do not become the operator/guarantor.
- Any future site must be validated by real customer counts, competitor order counts, price-band testing, and conversion observation, not just visible foot traffic or app monthly-sales screenshots.
- Avoid opening another branch until the owner can explain why the old store made money and why the two failed branches lost money in operational terms.

## Reusable Heuristics

- 老店赚钱 may be timing + location + old customers, not scalable ability.
- A failed branch is data. Do not dismiss it as only疫情 without isolating controllable causes.
- 人流 is people passing. 客流 is people with intent, time, and willingness to pay.
- For expansion, ask not "can I do the product?" but "why will this exact crowd buy this exact product at this exact price?"
- If transfer fee is not paid, the best rescue may be to stop before the first big irreversible payment.
