## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask total investment and where the money came from.
2. Immediately flag student loan/online loan as wrong funding for entrepreneurship.
3. Ask city, daily sales, gross margin, area, rent, staff, utilities.
4. Calculate daily/monthly loss and challenge why two workers are hired for 700/day sales.
5. Ask whether the owner is working in the store.
6. Ask why a new graduate did not first work/study before opening a shop.
7. Ask franchise location, franchise fee, deposit, material purchase, and equipment source.
8. Ask why this brand was chosen; reject "they promoted well" as irrelevant.
9. Inspect the site and identify old alley/old mall versus real current flow.
10. Ask future plan and debt repayment pressure.
11. Recommend closing or transferring and returning to work/learning.

## Calculation Logic

- 700/day revenue x 45% margin = about 315/day gross profit.
- Rent about 83/day + counted labor about 400/day + utilities about 30/day = about 513/day.
- Current daily loss is about 200/day, about 6,000/month.
- Even if one worker is cut, the shop still traps a new graduate in a low-upside, low-learning environment.
- The opportunity cost of staying one or two years in a weak shop is larger than the monthly loss.

## Diagnosis

The fatal problem is not only the store's P&L. It is a mismatch between life stage, debt, ability, and market:

- A new graduate used education/consumer debt to buy a first entrepreneurial lesson.
- The store is beside stronger competitors and not on real current mall flow.
- User does not understand brand, competition, site, or business logic.
- He is using "挺想创业" as a reason, but entrepreneurship requires accumulated operating ability.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Close or transfer the store as soon as possible.
- Cut unnecessary labor immediately while waiting for transfer.
- Treat the loss as tuition; do not add a second project such as street stalls before clearing the lesson.
- Use the current year as an应届生 job window. Find work, learn, build industry experience, and repay debt.
-创业 can wait until later; for this user the next task is training, work, and rebuilding confidence.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Student loans are for education, not opening stores.
- Young users with no operating ability should not use debt to buy franchises.
- A shop can be "not losing much" and still be wrong because it wastes the most valuable early-career years.
- "The brand promoted well" has nothing to do with whether the user's own store can survive.
- For fresh graduates, the first rescue question is often life strategy, not menu optimization.
