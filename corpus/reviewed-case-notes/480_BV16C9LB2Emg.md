## Calculation Logic

- Rent alone is about 103,000/month.
- Labor at 30 people x 6,000 is about 180,000/month.
- Utilities and other operating costs push fixed costs toward 350,000/month.
- At 50% contribution margin, break-even sales are about 700,000/month, around 22,000/day.
- With 120 average ticket, break-even needs roughly 185 customers/day before considering volatility, marketing cost, discounts, and empty-seat perception.
- A 860 sqm hot-pot store also needs visible crowding and table turnover; if it looks empty, the space itself becomes a negative signal.

## Diagnosis

The risk is not "can these founders work hard." The risk is structural over-sizing:

- First store has not fully proven payback, yet the second store is far larger and in a harder city.
- The site lacks parking and easy transit for social dining.
- The store depends on purchased traffic or destination traffic while carrying very high rent.
- Gross margin and quality assumptions are optimistic.
- Large area creates low人效, high management cost, high wastage, and weak氛围 if not filled.
- The claimed advantages are mostly self-belief: hand-made food, tea elements, local snacks, lower price, and classmates/investors. They do not yet prove a durable competitive edge in Shenzhen hot pot.

## Recommendation Pattern

- If still reversible, reduce area or renegotiate before opening. In the transcript, renovation is almost done, so 勇哥 frames the likely end state instead: the shop will probably be split into two stores/projects.
- Treat parking as a hard constraint for social餐饮.
- Do not assume office workers, university students, and residents will cross distance/friction for a big hot-pot store.
- Use the first store's real payback as proof. If 330 sqm in a lower-tier market has not recovered 800,000 after 1.5 years, 860 sqm in Shenzhen is not de-risked.
- If forced to open, track the first half-year ruthlessly: daily customer count, paid marketing cost, repeat rate, gross margin after discounts, table utilization, and cash burn.

## Reusable Heuristics

- "旗舰店" is not a reason to rent 800+ sqm. Size must follow proven demand.
- Gross margin from a public-company report is not the same as a small store's contribution margin.
- Social餐饮 without parking is structurally disadvantaged.
- Before expanding, ask whether the first store has paid back through a repeatable system, not through founder effort and temporary traffic.
- High学历/投资人 confidence does not replace store-level unit economics.
