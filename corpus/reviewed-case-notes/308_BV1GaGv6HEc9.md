## Yongge Question Order

2. Ask city, exact shop, current daily sales, and whether sales include delivery.
3. Ask gross margin, actual versus company-claimed margin.
4. Ask area, rent, staff count, labor cost, utilities.
5. Calculate daily/monthly loss and confirm owner salary is not included.
6. Ask when it opened and why the user did not ask before signing.
7. Ask total investment and break down rent, deposit, container/transfer, company fees, and decoration.
8. Ask who selected/verified the site and whether headquarters did on-site/video review or break-even calculation.
9. Inspect mall gate, neighboring shops, head-brand performance, night-stall competition, and whether the site is real客流.
10. Ask why delivery cannot be done and whether permits exist.

## Calculation Logic

- Sales 250/day x 50% margin = about 125/day gross profit.
- Rent 400/day + labor 226/day + utilities 60/day = about 686/day fixed burden.
- Daily loss is about 561/day, roughly 16,800-17,000/month.
- Because owner salary is not counted, true economic loss is higher.
- Even a rent reduction of several thousand helps transferability more than it rescues the operating model.

## Diagnosis

This store is structurally wrong:

- Rent density is too high for a 15 sqm kiosk in a third/fourth-tier city.
- The site has人流, but the kiosk is not a strong destination. Passing mall customers and night-stall customers do not automatically buy炸串 from this container.
- Delivery cannot be relied on because licensing/permit conditions are weak.
- Nearby successful head brands are not proof that an unknown snack kiosk can survive.
- Competing with street stalls while paying 12,000/month rent destroys the advantage of a small snack format.
- Changing product to ice cream wholesale or another category only adds new sunk cost; at best it may break even or earn wages, not recover 160,000.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Stop thinking about what product to change into. The product to sell now is the store itself.
- Package the store, brand/equipment/booth, and visible short-term traffic together for transfer to recover the maximum possible cash.
- Ask all friends and channels to repost transfer information; make the store look operational while seeking a buyer.
- If the landlord/platform can reduce rent, use the lower rent to make transfer easier, not as a reason to keep operating.
- Do not invest more in new categories, decoration, or equipment.
- Treat early consultation failure as the key lesson: once the shop is signed, options shrink quickly.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Mall-gate人流 is not enough for a kiosk if rent is high and product is ordinary.
- A small snack kiosk paying fixed rent must beat street-stall flexibility; otherwise it is paying shop rent for stall profit.
- If delivery cannot be done because of licensing/site constraints, do not use delivery growth as a rescue assumption.
- When fixed structure is wrong, "从哪里跌倒从哪里爬起" is just a slogan; judge by tomorrow's cash flow.
- A rent cut can be a transfer tool even when it cannot save the business.
