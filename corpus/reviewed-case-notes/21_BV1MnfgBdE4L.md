## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask city and category.
2. Look at the product model: table-side cooking, whether it is just-demand or social dining, and why it needs so much labor.
3. Ask daily sales, gross margin, rent, staff count, wages, utilities.
4. Compare current sales with rent alone: the store is not even selling enough to cover daily rent.
5. Ask total investment and where the money went.
6. Challenge headquarters' location evaluation and whether the user verified data independently.
7. Inspect the site: main flow, parking, mall relation, actual walking route, nearby competitor.
8. Test the product's repeat-purchase logic and whether it is just a short-term novelty.
9. Give stop-loss conclusion.

## Calculation Logic

- Rent alone is about 733/day.
- Labor after cuts is still about 667/day.
- Utilities are roughly 333/day.
- Fixed burden is already around 1,733/day before food cost and other fees.
- At 500/day sales and 50% gross margin, gross profit is about 250/day, nowhere near fixed cost.
- Even if sales rise to 1,000/day, gross profit is about 500/day; the structure still loses heavily.

## Diagnosis

This is a structural failure:

- High rent and high labor before demand is proven.
- Product model is confusing: not clearly just-demand, not strong enough as social dining, and too labor-consuming.
- The site borrows Wanda's name but not Wanda's real flow.
- Parking and stopping conditions are weak for social dining.
- The nearby better-performing competitor likely benefits from better position and novelty, not a durable model.
- Headquarters' PPT cannot replace real footfall and parking checks.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not "再坚持一下" just because it has opened only ten days. The daily loss is already structural.
- Treat the 420,000 as sunk cost; the live question is whether tomorrow's fixed cost should continue burning.
- If lease/transfer exit is possible, close or transfer quickly.
- Do not invest more promotion money unless there is a natural-flow or brand-power reason that can realistically fill the shop.
- Avoid this category/site combination in future: high labor, high rent, low repeat purchase, and no natural flow.

## Reusable Heuristics

- A location "near a mall" is not a mall location. Ask whether people naturally stop in front of the door.
- If daily sales cannot cover rent, do not discuss marketing first.
- Novelty products without repeat purchase need huge natural flow; a peripheral street shop cannot carry them.
- Headquarters' remote location score should be treated as sales material until independently verified.
