## User Facts Extracted

- Case type: planned/opening preparation, lease already signed, stop before further decoration.
- User profile: 40-year-old mother; chose site partly because she lives upstairs/nearby and children attend the school opposite.
- City: 武汉市区.
- Category: noodle shop with curry omelet rice / 重庆小面 style mix.
- Prior catering experience: none; says she can cook and previously did some home/kitchen group-buying for parents.
- Site: first floor plus second floor, about 95 sqm usable, roughly 45 sqm downstairs and 45 sqm upstairs.
- Rent: 20,000/month, deposit one month and pay one month. No transfer fee.
- Budget: about 150,000-160,000 total.
- Decoration: estimated 70,000-80,000; equipment about 40,000+; first batch materials about 10,000.
- Labor plan: five people, mostly family; counted at around 4,500/person, about 22,500/month.
- Utilities assumption: about 5,000/month.
- Gross margin assumption: about 50%.
- Break-even: about 3,166/day.
- Owner expectation: wants to sell about 5,000/day.
- Demand facts: school students mainly have meals inside school; outside demand exists mainly in a short after-school window. Nearby community and office/apartment flow is smaller than owner imagines.
- Competition: nearby快餐/社区食堂, 老乡鸡-like chain, hot dry noodles, Mixue/tea stores; breakfast shop has proven lines.
- Current status: shop taken only a few days; no transfer fee; further decoration can still be stopped.

## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask what problem she wants solved: category, opening plan, or site.
2. Ask city, whether lease is signed, area, rent, payment terms.
3. Ask prior catering experience and why she wants to open.
4. Ask budget split: decoration, equipment, materials.
5. Ask labor plan and calculate fixed costs and break-even.
6. Ask expected sales and challenge 5,000/day with nearby real demand.
7. Inspect actual entrances, school meal rules, subway, community scale, and competitors.
8. Separate real demand from family convenience: living upstairs and having children opposite is not market proof.
9. Compare taste/price/environment priorities for breakfast/noodle fast meals.
10. Recommend stopping and subleasing before more money is sunk.

## Calculation Logic

- Fixed costs: rent 20,000 + labor 22,500 + utilities 5,000 = about 47,500/month.
- At 50% gross margin, break-even sales are about 95,000/month, about 3,166/day.
- The downstairs rent density is severe: if upstairs is valued at about 80/sqm, then downstairs bears roughly 17,000/month, or around 377-380/sqm.
- Nearby total meal market may be limited; even if the whole small商圈 has demand, existing shops already divide it.

## Diagnosis

The issue is not opening activity; it is wrong starting logic:

- The owner chose the site because it is convenient for her family, not because the demand was proven.
- She over-counts nearby schools/communities and under-counts meal-time windows and competition.
- She assumes environment/decoration can win, but for breakfast/noodles, taste and value matter first.
- High rent and high family labor plan make break-even too high for a first-time operator.
- Because no transfer fee was paid and lease was just taken, stopping now is still possible.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not continue decoration. Stop immediately.
- Sublease/transfer the shop quickly while sunk cost is still limited.
- Do not spend more on equipment, decoration, or opening campaigns.
- If she wants to cook, start from a lower-cost test or keep it home/private-domain based; do not rent a 20,000/month shop as the first validation.

## Reusable Heuristics

- "我住楼上/孩子在对面上学" is not a site-selection reason.
- For school-adjacent shops, ask exact school meal rules and allowed outside-eating time. A school across the street may produce almost no lunch demand.
- Calculate rent density for the usable first-floor area; second-floor space often inflates area without adding sales.
- If a bad lease has no transfer fee and only days have passed, stop before decoration.
