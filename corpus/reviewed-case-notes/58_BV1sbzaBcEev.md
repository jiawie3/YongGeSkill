## Diagnosis

This is a combined failure:

- Brand-channel diversion: user did not independently verify official contact.
- No local consumer recognition for the joined brand.
- Weak site and direct competition from real known brands nearby.
- Excessive headquarters charges for franchise, equipment, materials, and sign package.
- User did not verify direct stores, true accounts, franchise filing, or actual operating performance.
- After opening, the store has no traffic and no unit economics.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not keep operating normally. First send a formal termination/解除 notice to the company and preserve notice evidence.
- Photograph/video the store, equipment, materials, sign, contract, payments, chats, ads, promises, and site-selection process.
- Negotiate with the company after sending notice, but assume voluntary refund may be unlikely.
- Check whether the company has commercial franchise filing/qualification and whether it complied with franchise disclosure requirements. If not, file complaints through official regulatory channels.
- Coordinate with the mall/landlord about sublease/transfer/exit because rent was paid for a year.
- Use litigation only after the complaint/negotiation route fails or with professional support; do not spend blindly on lawyers before evidence is organized.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Visiting an office/sample room is not visiting an operating direct store.
