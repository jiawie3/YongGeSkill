## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask current sales, city, how long opened.
2. Ask gross margin, area, rent, staff, wages, and owner involvement.
3. Calculate daily loss.
4. Ask total investment, transfer fee, prior experience, and whether user knew 勇哥 before opening.
5. Inspect the street: whether there is night economy, foot traffic, nearby old stores, and competitor type.
6. Ask why she opened: saw nearby old stores queueing and wanted differentiated music barbecue.
7. Ask product advantage and whether the shop can be operated by the owner.
8. Diagnose capability + site + product weakness.
9. Recommend immediate stop-loss/transfer and possibly rent part of the space out.

## Calculation Logic

- Daily gross profit at 1,000 sales and 50% margin is about 500.
- Daily labor after cuts is about 890 when owner labor is counted.
- Rent adds another about 509/day.
- Labor + rent alone exceed gross profit by about 899/day; utilities, materials loss, and platform/marketing widen the burn.
- Monthly loss around 30,000 is consistent with the transcript.

## Diagnosis

This is not primarily a single product or promotion problem:

- The owner lacks catering operating capability for a 200 sqm barbecue/tavern.
- Product is not sharp: barbecue, 江湖菜, alcohol, music, live singer, but no unique repeatable selling point.
- The street does not have enough natural night traffic.
- The nearby old shop's traffic belongs to that shop, not to the user's new shop.
- The 100,000 transfer fee was likely overpaid.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Immediate stop-loss. Do not keep testing by adding more concepts.
- Transfer the shop or negotiate with a neighboring operator who wants part of the space.
- If a neighbor wants the second floor, ask whether they will rent it around 10,000/month; reduce burn while exploring exit.
- Return to the user's original work/strength instead of trying to operate a high-complexity餐饮店.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Neighbor queues do not automatically spill over to a new shop; customers may be destination-buying that specific old brand.
- A first-time operator should not start with 200 sqm, live music, barbecue, alcohol, and炒菜 all together.
- Transfer fee must be benchmarked against neighboring lease reality.
- If product, site, and owner capability are all weak, the answer is stop-loss rather than "改一改".
