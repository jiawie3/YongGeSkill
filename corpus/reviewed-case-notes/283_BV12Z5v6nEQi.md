## User Facts Extracted

- Case type: already opened, debt-funded franchise/supply-chain store, stop-loss.
- User profile: about 33, already carrying close to 1,000,000 debt; this store added about 300,000+ new debt/investment.
- Category: bread/bakery store; uses headquarters/factory dough, packaging, and frozen/finished supply.
- Opened: about one month.
- Current sales: about 1,250/day.
- Gross margin: only about 40% in reality, despite supply side saying about 50%; daily gross profit around 500.
- Rent: 4,500/month, about 150/day.
- Labor: three workers plus owner shift support; counted around 15,000/month, about 500/day.
- Utilities: about 4,500/month, about 150/day.
- Current loss: about 300/day before considering broader debt burden.
- Total store investment: about 270,000-280,000; company/franchise/equipment side around 150,000.
- Location: street-side area with car repair, tire, auto-parts, building-material style shops; owner argues there are communities behind/above, but 勇哥 says the actual frontage is a fast-traffic street, not a bakery demand point.
- Site-selection logic: owner relied on headquarters/others, watched nearby bakery data superficially, and believed community/nearby flow would translate.
- Prior experience: had done food/catering before, but not enough bakery/category/site discipline.

## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask debt and whether the store is debt-funded.
2. Ask current sales, gross margin, rent, labor, utilities; calculate daily loss.
3. Ask why margin is low and whether headquarters supply chain is expensive.
4. Ask total investment and what was paid to company/headquarters.
5. Ask why this category was chosen; expose "saw others making money" and friend/sales recommendation logic.
6. Inspect the location and challenge the claim that nearby community equals usable bakery flow.
7. Ask where the community entrances are and whether people actually stop in front of the shop.
8. Ask what can be changed; reject cosmetic opening activity as irrelevant without traffic.
9. Recommend closure and negotiation with headquarters for equipment/material/franchise-fee recovery.

## Calculation Logic

- Sales 1,250/day x 40% margin = about 500/day gross profit.
- Rent 150 + labor 500 + utilities 150 = about 800/day fixed burden.
- Daily operating gap is about 300/day.
- Because the store is debt-funded, even "only losing 300/day" is not small; it is borrowed money paying landlord, staff, and headquarters.

## Diagnosis

The store is structurally mispositioned:

- Bread/bakery requires either strong morning flow, community entry flow, or destination brand/product pull. This site has none.
- The actual frontage is an auto/repair street; cars pass, people do not naturally stop for bread.
- Headquarters supply chain keeps margin too low for the current sales.
- The owner keeps reasoning from imagined communities and nearby data instead of the actual stopping path.
- Opening ceremony or marketing cannot repair a dead frontage.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Stop operating and negotiate with headquarters: recover/return equipment, materials, and any refundable franchise/service fees.
- If headquarters selected or approved the site, focus complaint/negotiation on site-selection failure and delivered value.
- Do not move the same model to another site unless debt pressure is solved and the owner can independently validate category demand.
- Avoid emotional tactics such as banners before collecting contract, qualification, site-selection, and service-delivery evidence.

## Reusable Heuristics

- A "community nearby" is useless if the community's actual entrance and walking path do not pass the store.
- Low rent does not save a shop with no stopping demand and low gross margin.
- If a user is already heavily indebted, even a small daily loss can be fatal because it extends debt-funded burn.
- Friend/introduction/sales recommendation is not due diligence.
