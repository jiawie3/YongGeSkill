2. Ask current daily sales, first-month total sales, and gross margin.
3. Ask city, rent, payment term, staff, labor cost, and utilities.

7. Ask whether there is debt or unpaid decoration cost.
8. Ask who selected the location and whether headquarters knew the surrounding competitors.

11. Compare "saving franchise fee" with losing brand recognition.

## Calculation Logic

- 150/day revenue x 60% margin = 90/day gross profit.
- Rent 12,500/month is about 416/day.
- Labor is about 200/day before utilities.
- Gross profit 90/day cannot cover rent alone, let alone labor.
- If closed but lease not transferred, rent burn is still about 416/day; if open, loss is around 596/day.
- Because rent was paid for a year, the remaining rent value is the key recoverable asset: about 11 months, roughly 130,000-140,000.

## Diagnosis

This is a structural no-go:

- Milk tea has strong head-brand effect. A single unknown county-town brand has no reason to beat established neighbors.
- The user chose a cheaper franchise because top brands felt expensive, but the cheaper brand has no consumer recognition.
- Headquarters selected a location beside strong brands without proving a credible competitive plan.
- The couple lacks operational experience and is already stretched by family/financial pressure.
- Changing materials or doing another brand flip would require more money and likely deeper debt.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Do not continue operating. Put up transfer/sublease information immediately.
- Recover the remaining lease value if possible; rent already paid is the largest salvage item.
- Preserve all contracts, transfer records, payment vouchers, site-selection communication, training promises, and opening support records.
- Seek refund/fee recovery from headquarters for franchise fee/materials/equipment where evidence supports it.
- If headquarters threatens reputation/legal action, answer only with organized facts and consult legal/professional support.
- Avoid rebranding or category switching unless new investment is near zero and the couple can personally operate.

## Reusable Heuristics

- In milk tea/coffee/hamburger/bakery, weak brands beside head brands usually die before "operation" has a chance.
- Cheap franchise fee is not cheap if there is no brand mindshare.
- Paid one-year rent changes the rescue target: first recover lease value, then fight headquarters fees.
- If a store loses less when closed than open, closure is already a rational improvement.
- Site selectors who place a weak brand beside strong brands must explain the competitive logic in numbers, not slogans.
