## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask what the store actually sells and which space belongs to the owner.
2. Ask main product and force the owner to choose between scene, party, cake, or restaurant.
3. Ask daily sales, area, rent, gross margin, staff count, labor, utilities.
4. Calculate daily loss.
5. Inspect the rooms and ask whether the space can really produce enough revenue.
6. Challenge repeat-purchase: how often does one customer hold birthday/party events here again?
7. Compare this with ordinary restaurants that can create monthly repeat visits.
8. Ask about location and current traffic acquisition.
9. Break down staff roles and identify that staff exists because the store tries to do too many things.
10. Recommend a narrower model and immediate labor cut.

## Calculation Logic

- Current 3,000/day sales at 55% margin gives about 1,650/day gross profit.
- Rent 666 + labor 1,400 + utilities 166 = about 2,232/day fixed burden.
- Daily gap is about 582, or roughly 15,000-17,000/month.
- Labor alone is about 46% of current daily revenue, before rent and materials.
- If the business becomes a scene/package-rental model with 80%-90% gross margin and only 2-3 people, 3,000/day could become profitable.

## Diagnosis

The problem is positioning:

- The store wants to sell both scene and full餐饮.
- Birthday/party scene demand is low-frequency; it needs constant new-customer acquisition.
- The kitchen tries to cover Chinese, Western, Thai-style, bakery, dessert, drinks, and light meals, so no clear signature product emerges.
- The location does not provide enough natural flow to support a large, labor-heavy restaurant.
- Staff count is a symptom of the unclear model.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Choose one model. Do not run it as both a full restaurant and a scene/event store.
- 勇哥's preferred rescue path here: sell scene/packages, not a full restaurant.
- Convert rooms into time-based/event packages: room + hours + simple pre-set food/drinks/dessert/props.
- Use simple, pre-preparable food or outsourced/simple hot items; remove the need for Chinese/Western kitchen teams.
- Cut 9 staff down to about 2-3 people. The transcript's headline advice is to remove about 7 staff.
- Put owner energy into scene design, short video, livestream, Xiaohongshu/Douyin distribution, private-domain referrals, and booking conversion.
- If the owner insists on常态化餐饮, choose one cuisine/category and accept that the current location and oversized space are unfavorable.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Catering works because of repeat purchase. If a "restaurant" becomes a one-time event business, it must be operated like traffic and booking conversion.
- High labor is often caused by unclear positioning, not by scheduling alone.
- A pretty store with no clear product and no repeat purchase can be busy sometimes and still lose money.
- For event-scene businesses, gross margin and staff structure should look more like space/package sales than full-service餐饮.
