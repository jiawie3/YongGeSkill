## User Facts Extracted

- Case type: takeover of campus milk-tea shop, currently breakeven, fixable by brand/product reset.
- City/site: 河南南阳某县城, inside a closed-management vocational high school.
- User profile: about 29 years old, works inside the school.
- School facts: about 3,000 resident students; school is closed-management; outside delivery cannot enter, and students can only secretly pick up outside delivery in small numbers.
- Store exclusivity: only one milk-tea shop in the school; there is category protection for milk tea.
- Current sales: April 15-30 revenue about 7,900, roughly 526-530/day.
- Gross margin: about 40% after school commission/points.
- Rent/commission: rent 100/month plus school takes 15% of sales.
- Labor: two people plus some temporary student help; about 5,000/month.

- Current product price: very low, often 3-8 yuan; best sellers around 4, 6, 7 yuan.

- School food environment: canteen mainly sells big-pot meals/fast meals; there is a泡面/boiling-water area and a supermarket.
- Main problem: previous/weak brand has bad taste/口碑 in students' minds; students know the shop but many do not want to drink it.

## Yongge Question Order

1. Ask transfer price, city, whether the shop is in county or school, and whether the school is closed.
2. Ask current sales and recent total revenue instead of accepting vague daily fluctuation.
3. Ask gross margin, rent, school commission, labor, utilities, and calculate true daily profit.
4. Ask total takeover spend and whether money went to brand or campus access/transfer.
5. Ask student count, whether delivery can enter, whether students can buy outside, and how many do.
6. Ask whether other campus milk-tea shops can enter and whether category protection exists.
7. Inspect canteen,泡面 area, supermarket, entrance, signage, and visible demand paths.
8. Ask what products are allowed and what conflicts with canteen/supermarket.
9. Identify whether demand exists but brand口碑 is damaged.
10. Recommend product/signage reset first, and a stronger brand application if needed.

## Calculation Logic

- 530/day revenue at 40% margin gives about 212/day gross profit.
- Labor 5,000/month is about 167/day.
- Utilities 900/month is about 30/day.
- Rent is almost negligible at 100/month, but the school takes 15% before/inside margin accounting.
- After labor and utilities, remaining profit is only about 13/day, essentially breakeven.
- Because fixed rent is tiny and demand is captive, the case is not a shutdown case; it is a demand-conversion and brand trust case.

## Diagnosis

This is one of the rare cases where 勇哥 sees a recoverable opportunity:

- The scene is strong: 3,000 closed-campus students, outside delivery blocked, only one milk-tea category slot.
- The current sales are absurdly low for such exclusivity, which means either product/brand/visibility is bad, not necessarily that demand is absent.
- The old weak brand likely created a bad taste label in students' minds.
- The user is stuck in sunk-cost fear: dared to spend 180,000+ on takeover/weak brand, but hesitates to spend on the change that may actually matter.

## Recommendation Pattern

- Rebuild the shop's visible identity: remove weak/old-brand impression and make clear signage.
- Add allowed summer drink products such as hand-made lemon tea,冰粉/清补凉-style drinks, and other permitted cold drinks.
- Use the remaining pre-summer period to test new products and improve immediate income.
- Apply for a stronger, student-recognized brand if possible. The transcript specifically treats Mixue-like recognition as suitable for this school population.
- If joining a stronger brand takes time, submit the application early and use summer break for reset/renovation.
- Spend where certainty is high: captive demand + category exclusivity + recognized brand/product. Do not be cheap on the part that solves trust.

## Reusable Heuristics

- Closed campus + category exclusivity is a rare positive constraint. Do not analyze it like an ordinary street shop.
- If 3,000 students and one milk-tea shop only sells 500/day, first suspect brand口碑/product/visibility, not lack of demand.
- Rent can be tiny but labor can still eat all profit.
- The right question is not "can I add any product?" but "what does the school contract allow and what do students already trust?"
- Some money should not be spent, but some money must be spent; distinguish sunk-cost waste from high-certainty brand/product reset.
